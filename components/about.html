About

Hi, I’m a software engineer in Los Angeles working in ad tech. I spend most of my time writing code, fixing things that behave differently in production than they did in my head, and learning new ways to be patient.

My academic background is in computational chemistry, high-performance computing, and data science. Web development came later, mostly on the job, through a steady cycle of building things, breaking them, and eventually understanding them well enough to stop doing that.

Outside of work, I organize ultramarathon relays. This is nominally about running, but in practice it’s logistics, recruiting, van rentals, food planning, and keeping a group of tired people moving in roughly the same direction. I also spend a lot of time outdoors running trails, skiing when there’s snow, and listening to techno whenever it fits.

I’m drawn to projects that require some planning, tolerate a little chaos, and are more enjoyable once you’re already committed.
