# Produce Data Sources & Methodology

## Primary Sources Cross-Referenced

### CA-Local Produce
1. **CA Grown / Buy California Marketing Agreement** — "Eat the Season" calendar (californiagrown.org). Official state marketing body backed by CDFA data. Provided the backbone of available/peak month ranges for ~40 CA crops.
2. **Foodwise (formerly CUESA)** — Seasonal produce charts and seasonal narrative guides (foodwise.org). Based on actual availability at SF Bay Area farmers markets. Used for cross-referencing and for specialty items (green garlic, fava beans, rhubarb, etc.) not on the CA Grown chart.
3. **CDFA / CA Ag Classroom** — California Fruits and Vegetables Seasonal Chart (agclassroom.org). CDFA source data with peak vs. available distinction for major CA crops.
4. **Daylight Foods** — California Produce Seasonality Guide (daylightfoods.com). Commercial distributor in Milpitas, CA. Comprehensive list of ~80 items used for cross-referencing.
5. **CSA Farms CA** — Month-by-month California produce calendar (csafarms.ca). Additional cross-reference, especially for stone fruit timing and berry seasons.

### Imported Produce
6. **ProducePay** — Mexican avocado season analysis, mango market situation reports (producepay.com). Detailed month-by-month import/export data for avocados and mangoes.
7. **Chilean Fresh Fruit Association / The Packer / Produce Business** — Counter-seasonal fruit import data. Chilean cherries (Nov-Feb), blueberries (Nov-Mar), grapes (Dec-May), stone fruit (Dec-Apr).
8. **FruitGuys** — Tropical fruit seasonality overview. Mango global supply chain (Mexico Mar-Sep, Peru/Ecuador/Brazil Oct-Jan), pineapple (year-round, peak Mar-Jul), banana (year-round), dragon fruit, lychee seasons.
9. **The Kitchn** — Tropical fruit season guide. Specific data for lychee (mid-Jun to mid-Jul), guava, cherimoya, and other specialty imports.
10. **USDA Foreign Agricultural Service** — Avocado import data and projections. CA avocado season (Apr-Jul peak), Mexican imports (Oct-May peak).
11. **FreshPlaza / FreshFruitPortal** — Current mango and avocado trade data for 2025-26 seasons.

## Methodology

For each item:
- **Available window**: The months when the item can be found fresh (not from long-term storage) in CA grocery stores or farmers markets from the given source.
- **Peak window**: The subset of months when flavor, abundance, and value are at their best.
- Where 2+ sources agreed within 1 month, I used the consensus.
- Where sources disagreed by >1 month, I used the more conservative (shorter) window and noted the longer one where relevant.

## Items That Need Extra Scrutiny

These had less authoritative sourcing or more variability across sources:

- **Passion fruit (local CA)**: Limited data on CA-grown season. SoCal backyard trees and small farms produce Jan-May but it's a very small commercial crop. May want to verify.
- **Dragon fruit**: Season data is for imports broadly — specific month ranges for Vietnam vs. Central America suppliers vary.
- **Cactus pear / Nopales**: Local CA season is approximate — heavily dependent on SoCal microclimates.
- **Guava**: Both CA-local (SoCal only, small scale) and Mexican imports listed. The CA season may be generous.
- **Fresh almonds / walnuts**: These are specialty farmers market items, not grocery store staples. Included for interest.

## What's NOT in the Dataset

Deliberately excluded:
- **Dried, canned, or preserved items** (always available, not seasonal)
- **Herbs** (most are year-round in CA or trivially greenhouse-grown)
- **Microgreens and sprouts** (year-round indoor production)
- **Coconut** (year-round import, no meaningful season)
- **Plantain** (year-round import)
