# Data Sources

Authoritative references used to build and validate the seasonal produce dataset.

## Authoritative Sources

- **UC Agriculture and Natural Resources (UC ANR)** -- California crop calendars and harvest timing by region. Primary source for local season month ranges.
- **USDA Agricultural Marketing Service** -- Shipping point inspection data. Used to cross-check availability windows and confirm commercial harvest periods.
- **California Department of Food and Agriculture (CDFA)** -- Annual crop reports with county-level production data. Validates which crops are commercially significant in California.
- **UC Davis Post-Harvest Technology Center** -- Variety-specific storage life, quality indicators, and post-harvest handling. Informs storage crop availability windows and storage tips.
- **CA Table Grape Commission** -- Grape season data by variety group. Source for the extended California grape season (May--January).
- **CA Strawberry Commission** -- Strawberry season and variety timing across California growing regions.
- **CA Avocado Commission** -- Avocado season, peak quality windows, and regional harvest timing within California.

## Import Data Sources

- **USDA Foreign Agricultural Service** -- Import volumes and timing by country of origin. Used to determine when imported produce fills gaps in California supply.
- **Chilean Fresh Fruit Association (CFFA)** -- Chilean stone fruit, berry, and grape export windows. Primary reference for Southern Hemisphere counter-seasonal imports.
- **Mexican mango/avocado board data** -- Season timing for Mexican avocados (fills non-California months) and mango varieties by export region.

## Variety-Level Sources (Stone Fruit)

- **Dave Wilson Nursery harvest charts** -- Variety-level timing for peaches, nectarines, plums, cherries, and apricots. Covers early, mid, and late season varieties.
- **Andy Mariani / Andy's Orchard** -- Heirloom variety peak windows, especially for lesser-known stone fruit cultivars.
- **Frog Hollow Farm** -- Peach and apricot variety calendars with week-level precision.
- **Slow Food USA Ark of Taste** -- Blenheim apricot documentation, including the narrow peak window and historical growing regions.

## Coverage

- Current coverage: 208 items with season data out of 335 total ontology items (62.1%).
- **Strong coverage:** citrus, berries, stone fruit, leafy greens, root vegetables.
- **New coverage:** herbs, chile peppers, mushrooms (cultivated), key imports, squash varieties.
- **Remaining gaps:** edible flowers, nuts (mostly), wild mushrooms, exotic tropicals.

## Data Quality Notes

- All season month ranges reflect California grocery availability, not just field harvest dates.
- "Local" means grown in California.
- "Import" means sourced from outside California, including other US states and international origins.
- Peak months represent the window of best quality and flavor, not just market availability.
- Storage crops (apples, potatoes, squash) may show longer "available" windows than their actual harvest windows due to controlled-atmosphere and cold storage.
- Year-round cultivated items (mushrooms, some herbs) reflect indoor or greenhouse production schedules.
- Import timing can shift 2--4 weeks year to year based on weather conditions in source regions.
